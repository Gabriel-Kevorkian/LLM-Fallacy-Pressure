This paper shows that letting multiple AI models debate each other does not always improve accuracy — and can actually make performance worse. The researchers tested different language models (like GPT, LLaMA, and Mistral) on reasoning tasks and found that after exchanging arguments, models often changed correct answers to incorrect ones, especially when they were in the minority. Instead of fixing mistakes, debate sometimes amplified errors due to social effects like conformity and agreement bias (similar to “peer pressure”). Even adding incentives to prioritize correctness did not reliably fix the issue. The key takeaway is that multi-agent debate is not automatically beneficial — without careful design, it can degrade reasoning rather than improve it.
