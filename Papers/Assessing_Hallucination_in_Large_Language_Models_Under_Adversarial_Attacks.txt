This paper studies how large language models like Vicuna-7B and LLaMA2-7B can be intentionally forced to hallucinate—meaning they generate confident but false or harmful responses—by using adversarial attacks, and it focuses especially on how different datasets affect this vulnerability. The authors test two types of attacks: Out-of-Distribution (OoD) attacks, which use random or nonsensical tokens to confuse the model, and weak semantic attacks, which subtly modify meaningful prompts to mislead the model without making them look obviously strange. They evaluate these attacks using two safety-focused datasets, AdvBench and HarmfulQA, and measure performance using Attack Success Rate (ASR), which tells us how often the attack successfully makes the model produce the targeted harmful or hallucinatory output, along with loss fluctuations to see how unstable or confused the model becomes during the attack process. The results show that dataset choice matters a lot: AdvBench significantly increases the attack success rate compared to previous custom datasets, especially for OoD attacks, meaning the models are much easier to break under certain testing conditions. OoD attacks turn out to be more effective than weak semantic attacks because random inputs disrupt the model’s contextual understanding more strongly, leading to greater instability and more hallucinations. The experiments also reveal that longer attack prompts (more tokens) increase the success rate, suggesting that longer manipulated inputs give attackers more power to exploit weaknesses. Loss analysis further confirms that models struggle more with random OoD inputs, showing larger and more persistent fluctuations compared to the relatively stable behavior under weak semantic attacks. Overall, the paper concludes that hallucinations are not just accidental mistakes from training data but can be systematically triggered, that some datasets expose model weaknesses better than others, and that improving LLM safety requires testing across multiple adversarial benchmarks to truly understand and reduce these vulnerabilities.
